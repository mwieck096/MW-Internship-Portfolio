Internship Reflection

When I started in the Summer of 2023, I expected my time with CHAS Health to run out when Fall came around, and then I would have to search for a new job. I have come to learn that my work is needed and appreciated here. I am the one who images devices for company-wide use, but I did not know when I started my internship with CHAS Health that I would be trusted with so many significant tasks. The skills I have developed have been useful for my Visual Communication Design experience at Eastern Washington University. Alternatively, the skills I have developed in my academic journey have been useful during my internship with CHAS Health.

In design, I have learned the importance of collaboration and being able to see the other side, even if you disagree. The most important skill I have developed from both design and my internship is the ability to take constructive criticism. I had to hone that ability in User Experience Design One and got practice with that skill through my internship. It is hard to accept feedback at times, but practice makes perfect. A more design-oriented skill I have worked on both through my classes and my internship is working with code. With my experience in Web Design One, as well as AI + Design, I have come to appreciate the effort it takes to code. One of my main internship tasks involves running coded scripts. If something feels slower or if the script repeatedly fails, it is my job to notify my coworker who codes the script. Another important design skill that I have incorporated in my internship is a user-centric approach. A major project with my internship involved reaching out to a user, notifying them that their work-issued device is retirement age, and arranging a time to replace their device. Additionally, I worked with them to ensure that their bookmarks are transferred and that they have all the applications they need to do their job. This gave me real-world experience in working with a “client,” as design would call a user.

I have worked on skills that I would still like some practice with, such as networking, problem-solving, adaptability, visual communication, and conceptual thinking. These things take time and practice. I will need to invest in both to become a well-rounded individual.

I am a naturally soft-spoken and introverted person. Many people are, but the world requires us to be loud and extroverted, otherwise we will never be heard or seen. I am most comfortable in the Information Technology inventory room, which is quiet and separate from the rest of the Service Desk. I have put in the effort to get along with my coworkers, to be able to joke with them and help them. I have put in an effort for my work to be seen. If I had never put myself out of my comfort zone, I would never have had my work seen. My usefulness would be utterly depleted, and I believe I would be out of a job. Pushing out of my comfort zone keeps me employed.

I have found that I have the ability to get along with almost anyone. CHAS Health’s Information Technology department is full of people with very different motivations and personalities. I, the introvert, can get along with anyone. I have worked with CHAS Health since I was 17 years old. This was my very first job or internship. I am proud that I have been with CHAS Health since July 2023.
